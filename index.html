Book Recommendations

  • Nineteen Eighty-Four by George Orwell

    If you compare elements of the story to the current society, there is a troubling amount of resemblance. George Orwell's cautionary tale lives up to its name. Frightening, but mind-opening at the same time. How "truth" is nothing but an interpretation of the things we see. We humans like to believe we know everything because it makes us feel safe, where in reality everything we know it told. Our actions are based on our thinking and where does our thinking come from?

  • Tuesdays with Morrie by Mitch Albom

    A dying man's last words to his student. This book is enlightening as it points out issues in our society that requries addressing. A striking problem is that people are living mindlessly, repeatedly chasing things that society wants us to believe we need when we actually don't and in the process forgetting who we are as an individual. In the process of chasing fancier and fancier things advertised by society, our energy and soul is drained, leaving us mindlessly working as a replaceable gear in this entire machinery of society.

  • Fahrenheit 451 by Ray Bradbury

    451 degrees fahrenheit is the exact degree a book is burnt. It reminds us of what happens when we refuse to question knowledge and only absorb knowledge presented. The protagonist's wife is committed to TV programs, living inside her own bubble, being desensitized to human interaction, only receiving input from the television, and in the process, becomes brainwashed by the television.

  • Born a Crime by Trevor Noah

    Speaks of the trouble of a colored kid growing up. Filled with comedy, but at the core of all this laughter is the undeniable fact of oppression and unjust towards colored people. A children born during the Apartheid is a crime, and what accompanies that child is a life filled with poverty and crime. In this story, we will be able to see the incredibles humans can perform in desperation.
